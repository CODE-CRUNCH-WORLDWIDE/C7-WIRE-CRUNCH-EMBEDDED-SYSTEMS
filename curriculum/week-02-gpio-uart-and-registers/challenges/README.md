# Week 2 — Challenges

One challenge this week. It is harder than the exercises and lighter than the mini-project. Spend ~3 hours on it. Self-paced cohorts may skip it; you will hit it again in Week 8 (SPI / I2C / CAN / USB), and the bench muscle you build here pays off then.

1. **[Challenge 1 — SPI bit-bang an OLED](challenge-01-spi-bitbang-an-oled.md)** — drive a 128×32 SSD1306 OLED over SPI by toggling GPIO pins in software, with no `hardware_spi` calls. The hardware SPI block exists; you will refuse to use it, so you understand what it would do for you.

Challenges differ from exercises in three ways: less hand-holding, an open-ended path, and a deliverable that lives at the *protocol* level (not the register level). They are also where Week 2 stops being a tutorial about registers and starts being engineering about timing.
